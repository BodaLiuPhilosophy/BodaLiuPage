I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Philosophy at the University of Vienna. I received my BA in Philosophy from Northeastern University (China) and my MA in Philosophy from Sun Yat-sen University (China).

Since my undergraduate thesis, I have been working on Husserl’s phenomenology. In addition, I maintain a sustained interest in Kant’s practical philosophy, the history of the phenomenological movement, Confucian and Buddhist conceptions of mind and nature, as well as psychoanalysis and empirical psychology.

My current doctoral research focuses on the problem of the ego–body relation in Husserl’s phenomenology. I attempt to introduce “generativity” (Generativität) as a new analytical perspective to revisit this classical issue. This approach starts from generative phenomena—such as mother–child relations, educational relations, and the experiential structure of “home–foreign world”—in order to rethink in what sense the ego is bodily, and in what sense it can be considered “non-bodily.”

I regularly participate in academic conferences and summer schools in both China and Europe, where I present my research. I also publish in academic journals. All publications can be accessed on this website.

Beyond philosophy, I am involved in a German–Chinese translation project of a philosophical metaphor dictionary (Shanghai People's Publishing House), which aims to clarify the extent to which philosophical thinking relies on metaphorical and imagistic structures.

I am also a music enthusiast, with a particular interest in Baroque classical music, and I have recently been listening to American country music and K-pop.
